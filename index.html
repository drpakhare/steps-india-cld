Pre-trial process evaluation

What these diagrams do

Built before results are known — to explain mechanism, not just test effect.

⟳
Map causal mechanisms

CLDs make explicit how the intervention is theorised to produce change. Each arrow is a testable causal claim grounded in existing evidence from LMIC peer support trials and India-specific studies.

⚡
Identify leverage points

Feedback loops reveal where small inputs amplify into large effects (reinforcing loops) and where systemic constraints limit change regardless of effort (balancing loops) — the programme's high-yield implementation targets.

◎
Anticipate failure modes

Balancing loops — therapeutic inertia, economic barriers, SMBG cost ceiling — are structural constraints the intervention alone cannot overcome. Naming them pre-trial prevents post-hoc rationalisation of null results.

≋
Guide co-investigator review

This is a living framework for challenge and revision. Co-investigators hold site-specific knowledge about which arrows are strong, which are absent, and which need additional local evidence before trial launch.

Feedback loop structure across all diagrams

R · Reinforcing
Self-Efficacy Loop
Controlled outcomes feed back to confidence, sustaining behaviour. The core growth engine — present in both HTN and DM CLDs.
R · Reinforcing
Distress Reduction
DM-unique: controlled glycaemia reduces diabetes distress, restoring adherence. Intercepted via PAID-5 screening at sessions.
B · Balancing
Therapeutic Inertia
80–90% of India encounters without treatment intensification. Demands supply-side IHCI change alongside the intervention.
R · Reinforcing
Social Engagement
Members with controlled outcomes sustain peers. Selective attrition is the main risk — addressed by buddy systems.
R · Reinforcing
Complication Awareness
DM-unique: awareness of retinopathy, nephropathy drives sustained behaviour and linkage to screening. R3 loop.
B · Balancing
Economic Ceiling
OOP costs ₹9,014/quarter for HTN; SMBG strips absent from Jan Aushadhi for DM. Partially addressed via SHG linkage.

What's in the viewer

Click any card to open the viewer directly on that diagram.

The three diagrams share a common node set but model different causal depth. The HTN and DM CLDs represent the core intervention theory, traceable to published evidence. The eCES overlay models five additional programme components with an explicit epistemic gradient — PSG pathways rest on trial evidence; eCES add-ons on structural and proxy evidence only. This distinction is visible in the arrow styling within the viewer.
